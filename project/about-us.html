About Us

Brief History

The Effutu Municipal Education Directorate (EMED), situated in Winneba, Effutu Constituency, was established in 2007 under L.I 1860 as a separate entity from the Awutu-Effutu-Senya District Education Office. EMED operates within three circuits: Winneba Central, Winneba East, and Winneba West. Led by Mrs. Rosina Anderson, the Municipal Director of Education, the directorate comprises four key departments: Human Resource and Development Unit, Supervision and Monitoring Unit, Finance and Administration Unit, Planning and Statistics Unit. With 80 basic schools and 5 second-cycle schools under its jurisdiction, EMED is tasked with implementing pre-tertiary educational policies set by Ghana's Ministry of Education.

Mandate

To provide quality education to all children of school going age in the Effutu Municipality.

Vision Statement

Quality teaching and learning to all children of school going age in an enabling environment.

Mission Statement

To ensure effective teaching and learning through efficient management of resources to achieve better academic results.

Key Functions

  • Implementation of approved national policies and programs related to pre-tertiary education.
  • Monitoring and Supervision of both public and private pre-tertiary educational institutions to ensure standards.
  • Training and deployment of teaching and non-teaching staff, including managing their payroll, transfers, and promotions.
  • Implementation and supervision of school curricula and ensuring effective teaching and learning to improve education quality.
